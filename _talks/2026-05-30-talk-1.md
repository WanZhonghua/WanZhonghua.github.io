In this talk, I present a cluster-based framework for mapping white matter neural circuits using ultra-high-field diffusion MRI. By combining ensemble tractography with connectivity-driven fiber clustering, our method builds a high-resolution atlas of whole-brain white matter organization and identifies anatomically coherent fiber clusters as fundamental circuit units. Using this representation, we reconstruct six representative cortico-subcortical circuits with high cross-subject reproducibility, demonstrating the potential of the proposed atlas for studying human brain connectivity, structure-function relationships, and precision connectomics.
