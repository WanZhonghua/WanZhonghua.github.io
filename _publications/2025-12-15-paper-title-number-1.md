Subcortical structures play a crucial role in sensorimotor processing, emotion regulation, and memory, but their complex organization makes fine-scale parcellation challenging. To address this, we present a multiscale subcortical parcellation framework utilizing consensus graph representation learning on diffusion MRI (dMRI) tractography data. Our fiber-cluster-based connectivity representation improves anatomical accuracy and reduces noise, while 3D-SLIC supervoxel preparcellation maintains local coherence and mitigates dimensionality issues. By integrating consensus graph learning with low-rank tensor modeling, we ensure consistent subcortical parcellations across subjects. Evaluations with 171 Human Connectome Project subjects demonstrate that our method yields subcortical parcels with greater reproducibility and microstructural homogeneity, resulting in a 15−25% reduction in coefficient of variation across six diffusion-derived indices compared to existing atlases. This pipeline provides valuable tools for mapping subcortical organization, with potential applications in precision neuroimaging and the discovery of clinical biomarkers.
